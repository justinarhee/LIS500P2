Teachable Machine: Algorithm & Reflection

Introduction

With our class readings and the topics of intersectionality, technology, bias, ethics, and human interaction, this project explored the intersection of machine learning, facial recognition, and ethical reflection. Using Google’s Teachable Machine, we trained a model to classify facial expressions as either serious or smiling and built an accessible web interface to showcase its real-time prediction capabilities. Through this project, we hope to take a deep dive into the critical issues Joy Buolamwini raises in her book Unmasking AI—issues surrounding algorithmic bias, representation, data quality, and transparency. Our project, though small in scope, mirrored many of the ethical concerns present in high-stakes AI applications. This website shares our process, the challenges we faced, the lessons learned, and our personal takeaways from building and reflecting on this system.
